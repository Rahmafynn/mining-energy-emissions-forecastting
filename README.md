Energy Consumption & Carbon Emissions Modeling in Industrial Mining Operations (Damang Gold Mine)
This project analyzes the relationship between energy consumption and carbon emissions in industrial gold mining operations (Damang Gold Mine). It applies regression modeling and time-series forecasting to quantify emissions drivers and predict future CO₂ trends for sustainability planning.
Mining operations are energy-intensive and contribute significantly to CO₂ emissions. Understanding the relationship between energy usage and emissions is critical for:

- ESG compliance and reporting
- Cost optimization
- Carbon reduction strategies
- Sustainability forecasting

### Methods Used

- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
  - Trend analysis of energy consumption and CO₂ emissions
  - Correlation analysis

- Statistical Modeling
  - Multiple linear regression
  - Model diagnostics (heteroscedasticity, residual analysis)

- Forecasting
  - Time-series modeling of emissions trends
  - Future emissions projection under current energy usage patterns
 
  ### Key Findings

- Strong positive relationship between energy consumption and CO₂ emissions
- Energy usage is a statistically significant predictor of emissions levels
- Emissions show an increasing trend consistent with operational expansion
- Forecasts indicate continued growth in emissions under current energy patterns
